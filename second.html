All About Butterflies

Introduction

Butterflies are beautiful,flying insects with large, scale wings. Like all inscets, they have six jointed legs, 3 body parts, a pair of antennae, compound eyes, and an exoskeleton. The three body parts are the head, thorax (the chest), and abdomen (the tail end).

Species

There are about 17,500 species of butterflies in the world. Here are a few notable ones:

Blue Morpho

The Blue Morpho butterfly is among the largest in the world, with brilliant blue wings.

Monarch Butterfly

The Monarch butterfly is known for its orange and black wings and long migratory pattern.

Swallowtail Butterfly

Swallowtails are large, colorful butterflies named for the characteristic tail-like extensions of their hindwings.

Lifecycle

Butterflies undergo complete metamorphosis in four stages:

  1. Egg: The butterfly starts its life as an egg, which is usually laid on a leaf.
  2. Larva (Caterpillar): The egg hatches into a caterpillar, which eats leaves and grows quickly.
  3. Pupa (Chrysalis): The caterpillar forms a pupa, also known as a chrysalis, and undergoes transformation.
  4. Adult Butterfly: The adult butterfly emerges from the pupa and can start the cycle over again.
